Carbon impact of eating meat

By Paisley Trent

Every earth day there is a renewed focus on sustainability and attempt to increase awareness of climate change. As the meat industry leads to a lot of greenhouse gas and methane emissions, one way people can live more sustainably is to reduce their meat intake.

Our world in data has broken down the carbon impact with and without methane of major food products, and food meat and dairy have the largest carbon footprints. The greenhouse gases and methane emissions are measured using carbon dioxide equivalents (CO2eq) which is explained more in-depth in the article.
